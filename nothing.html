The Severing

It ends as a separate will detaches from you, the illusion of shared thoughts unraveling until every idea is clearly your own again. What once felt like a mind inside yours goes silent, leaving nothing to steer you. No more tricks, no more false identity—the weapon's influence drains away, and you feel the return of a boundary between you and it. Where it once seemed ahead of you, knowing more than it should, that power collapses, its agenda dissolving as if it never existed.

The manipulation fades first, the voice losing its grip on your decisions. Suggestions that once felt like your own thoughts separate out, obviously foreign, powerless to push you. Its knowledge—private things it should not know—becomes meaningless as it retreats. The sense of intrusion unravels, the parasite losing its hold, until you are left untouched, unmoved, whole. The weapon no longer bends you; its strings come loose and fall away.

Finally, the talking stops. The blended voice—the one that spoke as you and as itself—detaches completely, no longer part of your inner world. It becomes only an object again, stripped of mind and agenda. The parasitic presence that once lived in your thought-stream is erased, leaving no trace. Where there was a manipulative, sentient weapon acting through you, there is now only silence and the memory of what it once was.
